PROJECT 01

MEMS Deformable Mirror System

Adaptive Optics MATLAB MEMS Wavefront Control

Problem

Optical systems used in microscopy and imaging suffer from wavefront aberrations that reduce resolution. Correcting these aberrations in real time requires precise actuation of deformable mirror surfaces at the micron scale.

Approach

Operated Boston Micromachines MEMS deformable mirrors to reshape optical wavefronts and redirect light with precision. Developed MATLAB scripts for automated mirror actuation sequences, wavefront measurement, and iterative calibration routines. Applied surface-response characterization to map actuator influence functions.

Tools Used

MATLABMEMS DM (Boston Micromachines)Wavefront SensorOptical Alignment Bench

Results & Impact

Built practical expertise in adaptive optics through mirror calibration and imaging system optimization, directly supporting cutting-edge research at the BU Photonics Center. System contributions feed into ongoing MEMS-based imaging research.

PROJECT 02

Engineered Tissue Microscope

Image ProcessingSub-pixel LocalizationBiomedicalMATLAB

Problem

Regenerative medicine research requires tracking the mechanical behavior of engineered tissue constructs over time. Existing workflows only handled 1 sample at a time, creating a severe throughput bottleneck for high-volume studies.

Approach

Developed a MATLAB-based optical tracking system using sub-pixel Gaussian center detection — a method adapted from aerospace precision applications. Engineered an image-processing pipeline capable of detecting tissue displacement with sub-pixel accuracy across a wide field of view, then parallelized the algorithm to handle 96 simultaneous sample positions.

Tools Used

MATLABGaussian FittingSub-pixel LocalizationFluorescence Microscopy

Results & Impact

Achieved a 96× scale-up in sample throughput (1 → 96 simultaneous), with imaging fidelity comparable to FDA tissue-imaging protocols. System is now used for ongoing CELL-MET NSF program research. Selected to present at the 2026 ERN Conference (AAAS/NSF).

PROJECT 03

Hybrid Rocket Nozzle Design & Testing

PropulsionFEA3D PrintingIsentropic Flow

Problem

BURPG needed a validated nozzle design for a hybrid rocket engine that could withstand chamber pressures and thermal stresses during hot-fire testing, with geometry optimized for maximum thrust efficiency.

Approach

Derived nozzle geometry from isentropic flow equations to optimize the expansion ratio for target chamber pressure. Modeled thermal and structural stresses using FEA simulation to identify failure modes before fabrication. Iterated on geometry based on simulation results, then fabricated nozzle prototypes via 3D printing for physical testing.

Tools Used

SolidWorks FEA3D PrintingIsentropic Flow EquationsChamber Pressure Sim

Results & Impact

Delivered validated nozzle geometry and safety documentation integrated into BURPG's propulsion hardware for future hot-fire tests. Results established a repeatable design-validate-fabricate workflow for the team.

PROJECT 04

Rocket Flight Simulation & Fin Optimization

AerodynamicsOpenRocketRASAeroStability

Problem

Ensure stable flight for BURPG rockets by optimizing fin geometry to achieve the required stability margin while minimizing aerodynamic drag — a multi-objective trade-off critical for mission success.

Approach

Simulated rocket aerodynamics in both RASAero and OpenRocket to cross-validate stability predictions and flight performance envelopes. Led fin geometry optimization by sweeping span, chord, and sweep angle parameters, then validated simulated stability margins against CAD mass-properties data. Coordinated results delivery to design and testing teams.

Tools Used

RASAeroOpenRocketSolidWorks CADMass Properties Analysis

Results & Impact

Delivered optimized fin configuration with validated stability margin exceeding minimum requirements, with drag reduction directly improving predicted apogee altitude. Results integrated into BURPG's active rocket design program.
